What dependent type theory cannot say, what it says only awkwardly, and how practice copes — trying to overclaim in neither direction.

Calibration first, because “inexpressible in Lean” claims are usually wrong. The independence of the continuum hypothesis — a statement about set theory, proved by forcing — was fully formalized in Lean by Han and van Doorn (Flypitch, CPP 2020): models of ZFC are built inside Lean and treated like any other structure. Measure theory, condensed mathematics, large-scale category theory are in Mathlib. The genuine limits are few and structural.

Hard limit 1: no quantification over universes

Part 1 introduced the tower Prop, Type, Type 1, … A definition may be universe polymorphic — valid at every level \(u\) — but the polymorphism is schematic (the reference manual’s word): a template instantiated per level from outside the logic. Internally there is no proposition “for all universes \(u\)”, and no “\(\exists u\)”. This is precisely the disease HOL had with dimensions — real^N schematic in the type variable \(N\) — moved one floor up: dependent type theory relocated the schematism; it did not eliminate it. Concretely: there is no single object “the category of all sets”, only one category Set.{u} of \(u\)-small sets per level, and a theorem about “all sizes at once” is a scheme, not a statement. In practice Mathlib states results at arbitrary fixed levels and this covers essentially everything — but the internal statement does not exist. Set theory meets the same phenomenon at proper classes; the difference is where the boundary sits and how often one reaches it (Grothendieck-style arguments reach it earliest).

Hard limit 2: a known ceiling on proof strength

Mario Carneiro’s “The Type Theory of Lean” (2019) determines the strength exactly: Lean is consistent provided ZFC plus “there exist \(n\) inaccessible cardinals” (one instance per finite \(n\)) is, and this is optimal — the universes are the inaccessibles. So mathematics essentially requiring stronger large-cardinal axioms is provable only after adding them (they remain stateable, as hypotheses about internal models — the situation of ZFC itself, at a specified height). For mainstream mathematics the ceiling is remote: whether even Fermat’s Last Theorem needs universes was a real question (MathOverflow 35746); McLarty’s analysis (2010–2020) founds Grothendieck’s apparatus far lower, and the Lean FLT project proceeds without foundational incident.

Expressible, at a price

Identifications mathematicians make silently

Where a mathematician says “canonically isomorphic — identify them”, set theory often allows the identification to be literal; dependent type theory makes the map explicit and carries it. Buzzard treats this seriously as a mismatch of languages — “Equality part 3” and “Grothendieck’s use of equality”: the working “=” often means “≅ via the map everyone has in mind”, which has, in his phrase, several definitions and no definition. Lean forces the map into the open. Occasionally this exposes a real gap in a paper proof; mostly it is a tax. The exercise below is the smallest instance: \(v \in \mathbb{R}^{n+m}\) used as an element of \(\mathbb{R}^{m+n}\), first as the type error it is, then repaired by transport.

Nothing is a subset of anything

\(\mathbb{N}, \mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Q}, \mathbb{R}, \mathbb{C}\) are five types related by injections, inserted automatically as coercions (displayed (n : ℝ) or ↑n); statements mixing them fill with casts, and proofs acquire cast-manipulation steps, mechanized by norm_cast and push_cast but visible. Subsets likewise: “the positive reals” is a new type {x : ℝ // 0 < x}, and structure moves along the inclusion by lemmas, not silently. Set theory does this genuinely better — at the price of junk questions.

Constructions by increasing union

Transfinite constructions that grow a structure by unions of a chain have no literal counterpart: types of different stages cannot be unioned, only glued along explicit maps. All expressible (Mathlib has ordinals and a large category-theory library), but what a set theorist writes in one line can become a design exercise. Quotients, by contrast, are smoother in Lean than in its relatives: they are primitive in the kernel rather than simulated.

What did not make the list

Classical logic (Lean assumes choice and excluded middle), analysis (the Part 1 theorem generalizes in Mathlib to ContinuousAt.comp over topological spaces), and concrete mathematics of essentially any kind. The limits above are real, and mostly off to the side of what most mathematicians do.

Summary

Dependent types put mathematics into the grammar. Gained: a checker that catches mathematical rather than clerical errors, and quantifiers, spaces, and structures from one mechanism. Paid: two notions of equality, explicit transport across silent identifications, schematic universes, and a fixed (if generous) proof-strength ceiling. Set theory and simple type theory make the opposite trades, and mathematics has been formalized at scale in all three; the comparison is an engineering judgment on which these notes have tried to supply the data.
